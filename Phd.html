PhD (Computer Science)

PhD is the highest degree awarded in an academic discipline and is the most satisfying and rewarding educational experience. Students work closely with a faculty member, performing original research, and tackling challenging and unsolved problems.

Research Groups

Human Information Interaction

This research group investigates all aspects of information usage by humans including seeking behavior, interaction techniques, storage/retrieval frameworks, and services for human information needs.

Contact: Dr. Shuaib Karim (skarim at qau dot edu dot pk)

Knowledge Engineering

Focuses on data/knowledge analysis using mining algorithms. Topics include Data Management, Social Computing, NLP, Vision, Testing, and Software Re-engineering.

Contact: Dr. Onaiza Maqbool (oniaza at qau dot edu dot pk)

Networking and Communication

Applied research in Networking, IoT, SDN, Cloud, Privacy, Smart Cities, and Distributed Systems with focus on security and modeling.

Contact: Dr. Muazzam Khattak (muazzam.khattak at qau dot edu dot pk)

FAQs

When will PhD admissions open?

Admissions are announced twice a year (Spring and Fall semesters).

What is the format and duration of the admission test?

The test is subjective, covering core areas of CS, lasting 120–180 minutes.

How can I apply for PhD?

You can apply online: ugadmissions.qau.edu.pk

Is it a full-time program?

Yes, PhD is a full-time commitment.

What’s the fee structure?

Visit: qau.edu.pk/phd-fee-structure

Do I need NOC if employed?

Yes, NOC is required at application submission and study leave after admission.

What about course work and comprehensive exam?

18 credit hours preferably in the first year. A comprehensive exam will follow.

Is synopsis submission required?

Yes, it helps match you with a suitable supervisor.
